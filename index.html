Dance Forms

Bharatnatyam

Origin-Tamil Nadu

Bharatanatyam is a temple dance and it tells stories from various Hindu scriptures.The foundations of Bharatanatyam are present in Natya Shastra, the ancient Sanskrit Hindu text about performing arts

Kathak

Origin-Uttar Pradesh

Kathak is a mix of worshipping deities and story narration. Grace, hand gestures and body pose as well as eye movements combine to make Kathak a graceful sight.

Kathakali

Origin-Kerala

Kathakali is a blend of devotion, drama, music as well as dance.The makeup in Kathakali is very significant. The 'Pacha' or green facial makeup represents a noble character. While 'Kathi' and 'Kari'means a villain as well as a demon respectively.

Kuchipudi

Origin-Andhra pradesh

This Indian dance form gets its name from the Kuchipudi village in the state of Andhra Pradesh. Like other classical dance forms on this list, Kuchipudi is also a temple dance.

Manipuri

Origin-Manipur

Hailing from the state of Manipur, this dance form is based on the 'Raasleela' trope where Radha and Lord Krishna share a dance.

Mohiniyattam

Origin-Kerala

Combining dance, drama as well as rigorous footwork, Mohiniyattam is a classical dance native to Kerala. Its name comes from two words Mohini and aattam meaning the graceful body movements of a woman.

Odissi

Origin-Odisha

Originating from Odisha, this dance form like Bharatanatyam is also a temple dance form. Odissi is popular worldwide for its lyricism. The'bhakti bhava' aspect, of an Odissi performance, is surreal.

Sattriya

Origin-Assam

This famous dance form is from Assam. There are several different forms of Sattriya such as Mati Akhara, Jhumura, Krishna Nritya, Nadubhangi, Gopi Prabesh, Gosain Prabesh, Chali, etc.
